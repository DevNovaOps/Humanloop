Sustainable Development Goal 1: No Poverty

SDG 1 aims to end poverty in all its forms everywhere by 2030. As of 2024, approximately 700 million people still live in extreme poverty (below $2.15 per day), with significant concentrations in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia. The goal encompasses income poverty, multidimensional poverty (lack of access to health, education, housing), and vulnerability to shocks.

Targets include: eradicating extreme poverty; reducing by half the proportion of men, women, and children living in poverty; implementing social protection systems; ensuring equal rights to economic resources; building resilience to climate and disaster risks; and mobilizing resources for poverty eradication. Progress requires inclusive economic growth, productive employment, social protection floors, and targeted programs for the most vulnerable—including smallholder farmers, informal workers, women, and persons with disabilities.

For social impact initiatives, SDG 1 emphasizes: livelihood diversification; financial inclusion and microfinance; conditional and unconditional cash transfers; skills training and decent work; land tenure security; and disaster risk reduction. Impact measurement should track both income and multidimensional poverty indices. Partnerships with governments, NGOs, and private sector are essential. Key indicators: poverty headcount ratio; proportion of population covered by social protection; and proportion of population living in households with access to basic services.
