Sustainable Development Goal 7: Affordable and Clean Energy

SDG 7 aims to ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all. As of 2024, nearly 760 million people lack electricity, and 2.4 billion rely on harmful fuels for cooking. Energy access is foundational for health, education, livelihoods, and climate action. The goal targets universal electrification, increased renewable energy share, and improved energy efficiency.

Targets include: universal access to affordable, reliable, and modern energy services; substantially increasing the share of renewable energy in the global energy mix; doubling the global rate of improvement in energy efficiency; enhancing international cooperation for clean energy technology; and expanding infrastructure and upgrading technology for supplying modern and sustainable energy in developing countries. Renewable energy costs have fallen sharply, making solar and wind increasingly viable for off-grid and mini-grid solutions.

For social impact projects, SDG 7 considerations: decentralized renewable energy (solar home systems, mini-grids) for rural electrification; clean cooking solutions (LPG, improved cookstoves, biogas) to replace biomass; productive use of energy (irrigation, agro-processing, cold storage); and community ownership models. Mini-grids and pay-as-you-go solar have proven effective in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia. Partnerships with utilities, private sector, and development finance are critical. Indicators: proportion of population with electricity access; renewable energy share; and energy intensity.
