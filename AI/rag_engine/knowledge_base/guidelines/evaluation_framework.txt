Guidelines: Impact Evaluation Framework for Social Innovation

A rigorous evaluation framework strengthens credibility and informs scaling decisions. Core components: (1) Baseline assessment—collect data before intervention on outcome variables, demographics, and potential confounders. (2) Comparison group—where feasible, use randomized controlled trials (RCTs) or quasi-experimental designs (difference-in-differences, propensity score matching) to attribute impact to the intervention. (3) Process evaluation—track implementation fidelity, reach, and barriers. Understand why and how the intervention works or fails.

Output indicators measure direct deliverables (e.g., trees planted, trainings conducted). Outcome indicators measure changes in behavior or conditions (e.g., adoption of practices, income change). Impact indicators measure long-term welfare (e.g., poverty reduction, health improvement). Use validated scales where available. Disaggregate by gender, age, location, and vulnerability.

Data collection: balance rigor with practicality. Mobile surveys, administrative data, and satellite imagery can reduce cost. Invest in enumerator training and data quality checks. Analysis: pre-specify primary outcomes; report confidence intervals; conduct subgroup analyses; document attrition. Ethics: informed consent, data protection, do-no-harm. Disseminate findings to beneficiaries, partners, and policymakers. Pilot case studies (Tanzania solar, Uttar Pradesh advisory, Lagos waste) demonstrate varied evaluation approaches—from RCTs to before-after comparisons. Align with SDG indicator frameworks for comparability.
