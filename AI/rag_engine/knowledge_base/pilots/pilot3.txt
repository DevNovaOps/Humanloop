Pilot Case Study: Urban Waste-to-Value in Lagos, Nigeria

A 2021 pilot in Lagos addressed plastic waste and informal sector livelihoods by establishing a waste collection and recycling value chain. The pilot engaged 200 informal waste pickers, organized them into cooperatives, and connected them to a local recycling facility. Pickers received training on waste segregation, safety equipment, and received fair prices via a digital payment system. The recycling facility produced plastic pellets for local manufacturing.

Over 18 months, the pilot diverted 850 tons of plastic from open dumps and waterways. Waste pickers' average daily income increased by 35%. The cooperatives negotiated better rates and gained access to health insurance. Environmental impact: reduced plastic leakage into Lagos Lagoon; cleaner streets in pilot neighborhoods. Social impact: improved occupational safety; formal recognition of waste pickers; women's participation at 40%.

Challenges: fluctuating global plastic prices; competition from informal middlemen; limited recycling capacity. The pilot addressed these through forward contracts with manufacturers, direct buyer relationships, and advocacy for extended producer responsibility (EPR) legislation. Stakeholders included Lagos State Waste Management Authority, a European development agency, and local plastic manufacturers. Key success factors: treating waste pickers as partners, not beneficiaries; transparent pricing; and integrating recycling with municipal solid waste planning. The model is being adapted for other Nigerian cities.
