EduRise Africa – NGO Profile

EduRise Africa is an education-focused NGO launched in 2010 with the mission of improving learning outcomes for marginalized children in East and West Africa. Operating in Kenya, Tanzania, Ghana, and Nigeria, EduRise has reached 800,000 students through teacher training, digital learning tools, and school infrastructure projects. The organization focuses on foundational literacy and numeracy, especially in multilingual contexts.

EduRise's flagship program, ReadWriteNow, provides structured pedagogy training to primary school teachers and distributes culturally relevant reading materials in local languages. Randomized evaluations have shown significant gains in reading comprehension and math scores. The TechClass initiative equips rural schools with low-cost tablets preloaded with offline educational content, reaching 2,000 schools to date. EduRise also runs a Girls in STEM mentorship program connecting secondary school students with female engineers and scientists.

The organization partners with national education ministries, J-PAL, and philanthropic foundations. EduRise publishes annual learning assessment reports and advocates for evidence-based education policy. They maintain a network of 50,000 trained teachers and have developed open educational resources in Swahili, Hausa, and Twi. Annual budget: $6.5 million. Headquarters in Lagos; operations in Nairobi and Accra. Contact: partnerships@edurise-africa.org.
