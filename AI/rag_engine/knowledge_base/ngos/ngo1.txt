GreenFuture Foundation – NGO Profile

GreenFuture Foundation is a non-profit organization headquartered in Nairobi, Kenya, with regional offices across East Africa. Founded in 2012, the organization focuses on environmental conservation, climate adaptation, and sustainable livelihoods for rural communities. GreenFuture has directly impacted over 500,000 farmers through its agroforestry and soil restoration programs.

The foundation operates three flagship programs: Tree for Tomorrow, which has planted 2.3 million indigenous trees across degraded watersheds; Climate-Smart Agriculture Training, reaching 120,000 smallholder farmers with drought-resistant crop techniques; and Women in Green Economy, empowering 15,000 women through beekeeping and organic vegetable cooperatives. GreenFuture partners with local governments, research institutions, and international donors including the Global Environment Facility and bilateral development agencies. Their monitoring and evaluation framework uses participatory methods and satellite imagery to track reforestation and soil carbon gains.

GreenFuture Foundation maintains strong community governance structures, with village-level committees deciding on species selection and land-use zoning. The organization has published extensively on best practices for community-based natural resource management and has advised national climate policy in Kenya and Uganda. Annual operating budget: approximately $4.2 million. Key contact: programs@greenfuture.org.
