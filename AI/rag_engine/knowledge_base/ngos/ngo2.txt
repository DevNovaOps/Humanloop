HealthBridge International – NGO Profile

HealthBridge International is a global health organization established in 2005, with primary operations in South Asia and Sub-Saharan Africa. The NGO specializes in maternal and child health, nutrition, and water, sanitation, and hygiene (WASH) interventions. HealthBridge has implemented projects in 14 countries, reaching over 2 million beneficiaries through community health worker networks and clinic partnerships.

Their signature initiative, SafeBirth Alliance, trains midwives and traditional birth attendants in rural areas where institutional delivery rates remain low. The program has reduced maternal mortality by an estimated 40% in pilot districts through improved referral systems and emergency obstetric care. HealthBridge also runs large-scale nutrition programs targeting stunting and anemia, including fortified food distribution and behavior change campaigns. Their WASH component focuses on household water treatment, latrine construction, and handwashing promotion in schools.

HealthBridge collaborates with Ministries of Health, WHO, UNICEF, and local NGOs. They use a results-based financing model and conduct rigorous impact evaluations, including randomized controlled trials. The organization emphasizes capacity building of local partners and has developed open-source training curricula used in multiple countries. Annual budget: $12 million. Headquarters in Geneva; regional hubs in Dhaka and Kampala. Contact: info@healthbridge-intl.org.
